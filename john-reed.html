2019 03 29 Publish

Anutin Charnvirakul’s Bhumjaithai (‘Pride of Thailand’) party featured his beaming face on posters emblazoned with green marijuana leaves during the campaign for the March 24 poll

In an interview with the Financial Times this week, Anutin Charnvirakul would not confirm reports that he had demanded the prime minister’s post in exchange for joining a Pheu-Thai led government. However, Mr Anutin confirmed he was interested in the top job

While Thailand’s two main political camps are trying to line up their next coalitions, Mr Anutin Charnvirakul is biding his time. He says he will not commit to either camp before the coronation and the announcement of final results in early May

A former senior official in Mr Thaksin Shinawatra’s party, Mr Anutin Charnvirakul was also described as a ‘close associate’ of Thailand’s new king by a US diplomat in a 2009 leaked WikiLeaks cable. Speaking to the FT, Mr Anutin said he objected to the notion of ‘democratic’ and ‘military’ political camps, which he described as ‘just wordings’

Mr Anutin Charnvirakul’s stock is rising not just figuratively, but literally. Shares in Sino-Thai Engineering and Construction, the family company he formerly headed - and which is building Thailand’s new parliament building - rose the day after the vote. Shares linked to Mr Thaksin Shinawatra’s businesses dropped

If Mr Anutin Charnvirakul is keeping his plans discreet, that is because a lot could still happen before the May 9 announcement of official results. Thailand’s junta-appointed Electoral Commission might disqualify individual MPs or even entire parties. MPs might be lured to defect to rival camps, as they have in past political cycles
