Desc With a career split into two distinct chunks, Otto Rehhagel was the great German coach humiliated in his own country by failure at Bayern Munich, who went on to transform Greece into the most unlikely European champions in history. Rehhagel made his name in Germany in the 1980s and early 1990s when he guided Werder Bremen to two Bundesliga titles, two DFB Pokals and the UEFA Cup Winners’ Cup. He turned Kaiserslautern into national champions after his Bayern disaster, but it was with Greece where his greatest achievement came - stealing the show with pragmatic brilliance at Euro 2004

2010 06 11 Retrieve

[The veteran coach, despite having won Euro 2004 with the Pirate Ship, will be making his first appearance on the sidelines in a World Cup match] Some things take their time. I’m pleased to take on such a huge challenge, and everything comes in due course. Things don’t repeat themselves in life. Ta panta rhei, as we say in Greece: everything is flowing.

[Looking ahead to his opponents, he said] South Korea are very far and strong - we saw them play twice in Austria, and they are very disciplined and tactical. It will be an interesting match tomorrow to see how they handle their strategies… Tactically speaking they have learned a great deal. In 1962 (as a player) I travelled through Asia and my team beat local ones 6-0, 7-0 and more, but those days are long gone

Asian teams have caught up greatly. They have movement that we Europeans can’t match. They run like panthers. They’re very clever tactically. We all know what football is right now and that’s down to you (the media) as you sent it around the world. As Asian teams learn well and are very disciplined, I’ve told my guys: ‘if you think for a fraction of a second that you can afford not to pay attention then you will be punished - badly’

[On his own role as a veteran coach, Rehhagel was cheerfully philosophical] Will my heart beat a little faster at the World Cup? My heart is going to be beating for a long time so of course it beats harder. I’ve done so much in my life so far but I’m so pleased to be here. It’s great to be in South Africa - it’s a fantastic country and the people are very happy to have this wonderful tournament here. Of course tomorrow my heart will beat faster as we face a tough opponent, and of course we want to win. You wouldn’t be human if that didn’t set your heart racing

2010 06 12 Retrieve

[World Cup 2010: Otto Rehhagel Admits Greece Were Second Best Against South Korea] We could have taken the lead, but we failed to score, and then we had a set-piece against us which resulted in the first goal. We weren’t careful enough. We were all there, but we didn’t clear the ball. We knew the Koreans would be very fast and would run the extra mile to get the ball. We tried to overcome that, but we never really got into the game. We were unlucky in a couple of situations towards the end of the match, but it was a deserved victory for South Korea

He is still young and he will grow. The English championship is now important to him. He can do anything in the future. He could be the national coach, coach at Bayern - anything he wants. All doors are open to him. I couldn’t score goals from the bench and Klopp can’t either. but if you have an absolute say, you can put your team together. Jurgen has the absolute say in Liverpool.
