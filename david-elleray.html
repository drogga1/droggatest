[David Elleray talks about handball] Deliberate handball remains an offence. In the past we’ve managed to improve the laws by focusing on outcome rather than intent. What we are looking at particularly in attacking situations is where the player gets a clear unfair advantage by gaining possession or control of the ball, as a result of it making contact with their hand or arm

[Elleray stated the introduction of the ‘natural silhouette’ rule was designed to prohibit defenders placing their arms behind their backs in fear of giving away a free-kick] We’ve changed it to say the body has a certain silhouette. If the arms are extended beyond that silhouette then the body is being made unnaturally bigger, with the purpose of it being a bigger barrier to the opponent or the ball. Players should be allowed to have their arms by their side because it’s their natural silhouette
