[Almeyda’s growing pains with the Earthquakes continue] The reality is I knew what I was getting into. I came into a project with a long-term approach, a team that only won four games last year, very few. We have to change not only the style of play, but also being able to change the mentality of every player. The first half we benefited from what we’ve been training, but the second half I didn’t like at all. In the first half we had two clear chances and if we had been effective the game would have been over. When they had that chance, they hurt us

That’s work, that’s mentality, it can also be concentration or lack of concentration. Of the nine goals we’ve given up I haven’t found one that the opponent has scored by their own virtue but by our own errors. That says we need to keep working to find what we’re looking for

[Almeyda is realistic] It’s not easy to change a style of play when a team is used to another style. We want to be protagonists and playing the way we played in the first half I think we can do that. And when we can do that for longer I believe we can get out of this hole that we’re in

For now we’re good. I’m doing a real deep analysis of the team in the middle of the season. For the moment we’ll try to give these players confidence

We played two games in one, the first was excellent, and the second was very bad, against a rival that doesn’t forgive you. A rival that has been working on its project for five years, that has their style of play installed. We’re looking for all that. Looking for wins, looking for a style of play, looking to change a mentality and it’s going to take time
