2019 11 28 Retrieve

[Inspirational Max Taylor set for Man Utd debut after winning cancer battle] My first thought was: ‘Will I play football again?’ But then you get to the point when you’re by yourself, you can’t sleep and all you’re thinking is: ‘This could end my life’

[After further scans, though, it was revealed that Taylor had testicular cancer] My mum broke down straight away. I was so taken aback I didn’t cry. I got out of the clinic and thought: ‘Oh my God’.

[However, he still had to undergo an operation because of swollen lymph nodes] After the operation I spent five nights in hospital, with an epidural pump in my back and all these wires that were slowly taken out. I still felt ill. I was still bald. I would look in the mirror and think: ‘I do not look good’. It was a good three months before I started to feel right

[New boss Ole Gunnar Solskjaer then invited him to watch the first team train] It was a great way to come back, very uplifting. That was massive for me. I thought people might not remember

[Taylor said of his illness] I don’t want it to define me
