[The story of Liverpool, Roma and the European Cup final of 1984] It’s the greatest night of my career. You got used to winning trophies at Liverpool, but to know at the end of the season that you were the best team in Europe? That was a step above, it felt like we were part of the elite

It was some occasion. You probably don’t appreciate it at the time, but looking back you do. Beating a team in front of their own fans in a European Cup final? It’s some achievement

The aim at the start of every season was the same. You want a trophy, two if possible, all of them if you can. We never said one was more important than the other, we just went for the lo

We were a top side. We rarely worried about the opposition. We just went out and played. And if we played, we reckoned nobody would stop us

We seemed to perform better away from home that season.

People always ask about being intimidated playing Roma. But that Bucharest tie was 10 times more scary. Souness had broken their best player’s jaw in the first leg at Anfield, so they wanted to kill him over there. They wanted to kill all of us!
