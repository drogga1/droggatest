2017 06 14 Retrieve

[Described as a politician in a hurry to the top in recent times] Leo Varadkar was in a big hurry to go to university. So he wanted to skip that year and go to Leeson Street. But he was talked out of it

[Asked if anything triggered Leo Varadkar’s interest in politics, Miriam Varadkar points to Ashok Varadkar and said their son used to sit watching all the political TV programmes with his father] From about the age of ten, he used to watch all the elections with Ashok, he was very keen on what was happening in other countries

[Miriam recounts a stand out moment in the 1980s when a shopkeeper asked Leo Varadkar what he wanted to be when he grows up] He said he wanted to be Minister for Health and it was very embarrassing as I think he was only about seven or eight. I was actually shocked

[About Leo who risen through the ranks to the office of Taoiseach] We are proud as punch. We are delighted for him because we think it is probably what he wants. We are happy but we still get worried a bit now and again. I am more concerned about the media because I think that they love you some weeks and after a few weeks they find a lot of faults and they tear you down. [She concluded] We are delighted. We are very proud and we hope that the country will be very proud of him
