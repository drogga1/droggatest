To make the country more competitive, the next government must decentralise and devolve state power to more local levels, giving people more say in political decision-making, says Democrat Party leader Abhisit Vejjajiva.

Abhisit Vejjajiva said Thailand faces many challenges such as an ageing society, economic inequality and technological disruption so the country needs effective policies to deal with these problems.

Mr Abhisit Vejjajiva urged the next government – expected to come to power after the election next year – to empower private companies, especially in the IT sector, by eliminating outdated regulations which prevent them from providing new services and products

Mr Abhisit also said the Democrat Party now has clear differences in policies: First, the party believes in democracy; secondly, it believes in decentralisation; and thirdly it thinks a new paradigm is needed for economic management
