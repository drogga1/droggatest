[Former Manchester United goalkeeper Roy Carroll has recalled details from the two-hour conversation he had with Sir Alex Ferguson before he joined the club] was very close to signing for Leicester when I was at Wigan. Everything was agreed. We had met and agreed the next day I would sign. But then in the end their coach, Peter Taylor, told me they wanted a more experienced goalkeeper and they signed Ian Walker. I was very upset, but that’s what happens in football and then a few days later Sir Alex Ferguson called. I couldn’t believe it. We talked on the phone for two hours. He told me that they had been watching me for the last two years. I was very nervous at the time, but also so happy, at 23 years old. I was talking to the best coach in the world. After I agreed, I would meet my new team-mates three weeks later. Until that day came, I couldn’t sleep

The fact that I went through Manchester United was a great honour. We won the Premier League, an incredible feeling. I never expected it to happen to me

[Recalling his first time at the club’s Carrington training ground] He approached me and said, ‘Hello, I’m David Beckham.’ I knew who he was and I introduced myself. There was no reason, but I was nervous. But, they all made me feel very happy. I had a fantastic time. It’s a dream come true for many to play for United. We won titles, we had 70,000 spectators. every game. But, I didn’t leave with the best breath because we lost the FA Cup final to Arsenal in 2005
