The club’s general director Jose Angel Sanchez - Perez’s right-hand man - has been promoting a transfer policy that is not based on massive expenditure ever since the turn of the century. They are keen to bring in players and turn them into superstars where their values go up.

Real Madrid now feel they can’t compete financially with the money from Manchester City and those clubs backed by Arab countries. Lunin didn’t play much at Leganes and Valladolid but they have confidence that he will be a very good goalkeeper in the future

The situation can be compared to Varane when he first arrived. He arrived on Zidane’s recommendation in 2011. The feeling is that beyond Militao, Nacho is not going to stay as the fourth option. There’s a chance they will look to sign someone in this position

He has the complete confidence of Zidane but Sergio Reguilon - who has been on loan at Sevilla - is expected to come back. He’s another player who may be loaned out or sold. Zidane still thinks Marcelo can hold down the left-sided berth, but he also feels that at times Mendy is the better option. He was picked over Marcelo in the Champions League last-16 first leg against Manchester City and the feeling was he was going to also be picked in the second leg as he is more defensive-minded. The left-back is perhaps the only position where there’s not a clear first and second choice

[Montero would be surprised if either Soumare or Kante were to form part of Zidane’s rebuild] It would be strange for me if Madrid were to sign Kante as he’s 29. The three famous players who helped give Zidane three successive Champions League titles - Casemiro, Kroos and Modric - are amazing but age is now a problem. Zidane has not always used Modric, with Valverde coming in. We’ve seen the first transition, and in this next year, we will see more an evolution in the midfield

Zidane’s obsession is to sign Pogba. There is plenty of debate in Spain already about Valverde being better than Pogba, but he’s a world champion, he’s only just turned 27 and he’s box-to-box. Camavinga is another option as he falls in line with the transfer strategy the club are wanting to follow

Then there is Isco. In the same way Zidane resurrected Marcelo’s Madrid career, so too did he bring Isco back from the dead. Before the coronavirus crisis, the feeling in Spain was that Luis Enrique was going to call up Isco to the Spanish national team. A year ago, that certainly wouldn’t have been the case
