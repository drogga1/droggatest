2015 11 09 Retrieve

New York City FC didn’t take long to fire Jason Kreis after a disappointing debut season, and they didn’t dally in hiring a replacement

They kept the job in the City Football Group family, tabbing Patrick Vieira, the 39-year-old former Arsenal great who has been serving as coach of co-owner Manchester City’s Elite Development Squad

Vieira will fly to New York on Tuesday to meet his new players and spend time with NYCFC sporting director Claudio Reyna and goalkeeping coach Rob Vartughian. He’ll have a three-year contract, and will have enviable resources and an unenviable mix of high expectations and an underperforming squad

But how well can an inexperienced coach who has never played or managed in MLS adjust to the league’s salary cap, draconian rules and NYCFC’s aging roster?

NYCFC missed the playoffs in its debut season despite a roster that included David Villa, Andrea Pirlo and Frank Lampard - who at 37 is just two years younger than Vieira - and a payroll of $17,675,368, the third-highest in MLS and one of the priciest in league history, according to Spotrac

He’ll start with NYCFC on Jan. 1, with Vartughian running practices until then and coaching on their Puerto Rico trip, which Vieira will take in as an observer. Vieira’s Elite Development Squad has won a Premier League International Cup and is atop its group in the UEFA Youth League
