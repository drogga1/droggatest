[Socceroos midfielder Mark Milligan has opened up on his heartbreak after a transfer to Championship side Bolton Wanderers fell through because of the FIFA world rankings] I was absolutely gutted with the final result from the visa board. I was pretty much done and dusted [to join Bolton] and was so close to getting my move to England. Obviously it dragged on a little bit in the end because we were sort of waiting for new rankings to come out in the hope it will push [the Socceroos] a bit closer to the top 50. In the end it didn’t but [the visa board] held out for that long and other [opportunities] passed by. A lot of teams make signings or make offers and when you turn them down, they’re not there days later.

Yeah absolutely. I work extremely hard on keeping my body right. Although I’m getting on a little bit now, I don’t feel like I’ve missed a beat since the off-season and coming back in pre-season

Hopefully [we can get through] these next two games - and we don’t know what’s going to happen after that - but [I’ll be] looking at possibly going to a fourth World Cup. There isn’t anyone at the moment that can say they’ve done that. That’s something that definitely drives me and although we fell short with the national team rankings, and it did hurt me, I still have a lot of passion and pride playing for my country. As long as I have that, I’ll be over the moon for any selection that I’m picked in going forward

2018 02 15 Retrieve

[Mark Milligan admits why he left Melbourne Victory for Al Ahli] I choose to take the move to Al Ahli obviously because of the size of the club. In negotiations they were very keen on acquiring my services and I think when a club is pushing that hard you know you’re going to play a role in what they’re trying to achieve. Obviously they have high expectations of the team and sitting second on the table, still in the cup and Champions League so I think the challenge of coming into such a big club in the Asian region was too good an offer to sort of look past.

[However in making his debut for Al Ahli against bitter rivals Al Ittihad in a 0-0 away draw, Milligan saw first hand the magnitude of support in the region] The fans there are fanatical. I was fortunate enough to play my first game in the derby against Al Ittihad and there were 62,000 fans there…it was unbelievable. They have high expectations on the club too, and I like those pressures to succeed

[Australian midfielder Mark Milligan has inadvertently gained some precious insight into new Socceroos boss Bert Van Marwijk] I think it was very important we [Australia] appointed a coach with such a reputation. 2019 11 06 Retrieve

[Mark Milligan on the Football Federation Australia’s ground-breaking deal that closes the pay gap between the men’s and women’s national teams] It’s been clear for everybody to see how much it [women’s football] has grown over the last few years, and especially how well our Matildas have been doing. I’d only really heard and read about the successes they were having, and to be able to go across and witness it first hand was extraordinary. The timing for me probably couldn’t have been better, because it really drove home how important it was during these negotiations that they got what they deserved. Going forward, what a value they are to the Australian footballing community
