2017 11 25 Retrieve

[Eugenie Le Sommer says she would trade all of her club honours to win the Women’s World Cup on home soil in 2019] That’s a tough one. I have to say yes. I’d take the World Cup because I can always go back to my club and win more titles with them. The World Cup is the most beautiful of competitions. It’s the stuff dreams are made of

2019 12 03 Retrieve

[Rapinoe’s Ballon d’Or 2019 blitz exposes big visibility problem in women’s football] UEFA spoke about asking players what they want to improve and what we think, to help them to be better. We discussed some [ideas]. We spoke about the pitch. We need to have a good pitch with a good stadium with some people inside. When I watch a game, even if it’s my league, if it’s a bad pitch, bad stadium, I will change [the channel]. To keep people watching, we need to have a good environment. This is important

[Referring to the quarter-finals of the Champions League which will take place in March] The next round? Wow. Before that, it’s like, how many goals will we score, you know?

[In Lyon’s round of 32 tie this season, they played Ryazan of Russia] I prefer to play against a big team. Even if it’s harder and we can lose. I prefer the feeling before the game, the adrenalin. UEFA has to work on the competition to have maybe a third team from England, France, Sweden, Spain, Germany directly qualify

[How about group stages?] Yes, why not? I like it with the men. We need to try another way and to have more competitive games because now it’s … it’s not boring, but for me, Champions League, it’s ‘wow’. We need to play big games. I understand also that the first round is easier, but some teams need to qualify before that
