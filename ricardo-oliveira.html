Ricardo Oliveira
Gender Male
Ethnic Brazillian
Job Brazillian Footballer
Desc A steady goalscorer in Spain with Valencia, Zaragoza and, most notably, Real Betis, Oliveira first broke into the Brazil national team set-up around 2004, serving as one of several understudies to Ronaldo. Usually deployed from the bench, there were a few highlights along the way for Oliveira, who also turned out for AC Milan as part of a nomadic career that took in spells with 10 different clubs - some more than once. Part of the Brazil squad that emerged victorious from the 2004 Copa America minus Ronaldo, Oliveira even got on the scoresheet in a 4-0 win over Mexico in the quarter-finals and started five of Brazil’s six games at the 2005 Confederations Cup, scoring twice

[Ricardo Oliveira on his time at Milan and the kidnapping of his sister] My head was all over the place. My sister practically dates me. She lives in my house, she works for me, I look after her. She’s like my mother. We’re so close. One day Carlo Ancelotti came to me and said, ‘Ricardo, I can’t take you to the games. If you want to keep on training, I think it’s good for you because you’ll be with your team-mates - we’re here to give you total support. But you already had enough pressure on you inheriting the No7 shirt of Sheva, who made so much history here - I don’t think it’s fair to put extra pressure on you by selecting you to play.’ I said, ‘Carlo, this is what I want.’ It was impossible to play football. She was kidnapped forfive months- the longest in the history of Sao Paulo at the time. But what was worthwhile at Milan was to be part of such a successful team, who won the Champions League with Kaka dazzling, the best player in the world. To share a dressing room with Seedorf, Ronaldo, Maldini, Costacurta, Nesta, Pirlo, Gattuso - a constellation of stars
