Desc Moore captained England to their only World Cup triumph in 1966, aged only 25, and went on to feature 108 times for the Three Lions. He remains the fifth most capped England player ever. Moore was best known for his reading of the game and ability to anticipate opposition movements, distancing himself from the image of the hard-tackling, high-jumping hatchet men so prevalent during his days
