Mesut Ozil has become known as the ‘assist king’ at the Emirates, and statistics from Oulala show that the German could yet don the crown for England’s top flight come the end of the campaign.

With 12 assists from Arsenal’s 15 games so far, the 27-year-old is on course to seal his coronation and break Thierry Henry’s 13-year-old record

The Gunners legend notched 20 league assists back in the 2002/03 season, leaving Ozil just eight away from equalling it with 23 matches remaining, and only six short of the competition’s second-highest total of 18 recorded by Frank Lampard back in the 2004/05 campaign

His performance equates to 0.86 assists per game in the Premier League, meaning that - should he play in all of Arsenal’s remaining fixtures - he could yet break the 30-assist mark
