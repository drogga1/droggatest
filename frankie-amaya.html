[Frankie Amaya continues rapid rise by becoming first overall pick in 2019 MLS draft] It was the longest four minutes of my life.

[Amaya admits beating Mexico in the Concacaf U-20 final helped instill a new level of confidence in him] That game meant the world to me honestly because I wasn’t really the best player on that team. I worked really hard during the camp to earn my spot and I just want to thank Tab for giving me the chance to showcase my skills on a team with mostly pros.

A final you always have to play your best game. My family is Mexican-American so it gave me a little more motivation. They were all wearing U.S. shirts back home and I didn’t want to let them down. Especially with Thanksgiving coming up. I didn’t want to go home a loser

A lot of people think that I can’t play defense but I’m just a work machine. People are more talented than me, but I don’t think anyone on the field is going to work harder than me, offensively and defensively. I like to get in tackles and stuff like that even though I’m a small player. I like to challenge every ball

[Ramos gave Amaya that opportunity in November] I’m not going to lie, I didn’t see this coming. I worked hard my whole life. Being a pro is something I’ve always dreamed about and I’m ready to give it my all
