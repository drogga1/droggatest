[MLS helping CONCACAF’s budding powers compete with USA and Mexico] You play a whole year against Altidore, Bradley, for example, and Dempsey. You play against good players and sometimes you beat them. You see that wall sometimes against these players, and when you win against them you say, ‘I can do it’. You take away [the thought] in your mind that you can’t do it. It helps a lot for the national team, for sure.

We used to always talk MLS - ‘It’s not that good for us, I’d better go to Europe.

In Mexico it’s only South American players - they don’t bring in any Caribbean, they don’t bring in any Central Americans. The MLS has open hands, like, ‘Welcome all of you, good players come here, enjoy the league, improve, develop, make the league better.
