2016 12 09 Retrieve

[Toronto FC’s Will Johnson ready to celebrate as he eyes third MLS Cup] It was brutally hard to watch it, but I knew I wasn’t starting so it’s not like it was a surprise. It was tough. I had put a lot into the Portland project, but it was nice to see all the hard work that I put into it, and did for that group, was parlayed into success. That, for me, was gratifying, but once the season was over I felt I needed a change. It wasn’t easy, it was a heck of a move [from Portland]. Family wise, off the field, it was brutally tough. I can’t understate that side of things, but from Day One you get to this [Toronto FC] club, this training ground, this city, you know this is special.

[Johnson’s belief in TFC was cemented even further after a chance encounter with Larry Tanenbaum] He knew details of my career that blew me away. He knew about my leg break, about my time in Holland. He was so in-depth with the details that my wife Caroline and I were blown away, and so that was one of the things that made me say, ‘This is different, this is cool’.

Every year that you play in this league, you see it grow and you see the quality of players rise, it gets harder and harder to win MLS Cup and they become more and more meaningful. The MLS Cups that were won in the late 90s and early 2000s were small numbers, and now we’re dealing with 20 teams in the league, major superstars, so every year it gets harder to do, and more special
