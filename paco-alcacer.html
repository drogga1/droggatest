2019 09 04 Retrieve

[Paco Alcacer believes that his Borussia Dortmund team-mate Jadon Sancho has what it takes to become one of the world’s best] He’s a kid, 19 years old, with great quality. If he keeps his head, he will be a top player. There’s uncertainty at that age, but as a player and a person in the dressing room, he’s top. If he improves and picks up other qualities, he will be one of the best in the world

[Asked if this is the most confident he’s felt in his career] It could be. The numbers suggest that. I feel confident and comfortable with my teammates – which is a plus on the pitch. It’s a question of confidence and feeling comfortable on the pitch. Minutes are important. The treatment from my teammates, having an understanding with them, that’s also key

[When asked if he would celebrate against his former club if he scores, Alcacer wouldn’t rule the possibility out] I wouldn’t know what to do, because at the end of the day you’re really happy [to score]. 2019 09 15 Retrieve

[Paco Alcacer has accused several people of treating him badly at Barcelona ahead of a Champions League reunion with his former club] I’ll decide in the moment. I have respect for many people there. Many people behaved very well with me but many people behaved very badly. My team-mates and the fans treated me well. As in any job, if people trust you, you give your best, and if they don’t trust you, you don’t give it. It’s very important for me to see those signs of trust and in the end many people did not show it

[Alcacer was asked about Messi’s influence at Camp Nou] It’s clear that a player of that level is important for the team and for the club. You will always ask for his opinion and listen to him. Hopefully he will play next weekend, against Granada, and not against us!
