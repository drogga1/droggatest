[Jurgen Klopp is Liverpool’s 12th player on the pitch] He’s a really good manager, one to one. He gives players confidence, he makes you feel very important. Whether you’re a player coming on from the bench or whether you’re a starter, everyone feels the same. So I give him credit for that. Jurgen is a bit more aggressive [than Eddie Howe], not in a bad way. He gets more excited! You can see how he is on the sideline as if he’s the 12th player on the pitch. He helped me a lot on the defensive side of things. I wasn’t the best at tracking back. It’s like that with a lot of wingers. It’s a lot more work off the ball, but I feel as if I’ve done better from the defensive aspect of things.
