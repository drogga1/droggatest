2014 03 09 Publish: Arsenal manager Arsène Wenger calls on Mesut Özil to concentrate on scoring as German whirs back into life

Arsenal manager wants more goals from Mesut Özil as £42-million signing scores for the first time in 17 matches during 4-1 FA Cup win over Everton

Relax, Arsenal fans: it was nothing. Like a lavishly expensive new cordless blender that whirred like a dream for the first few months before developing a series of mildly alarming technical glitches, the supporters whose season tickets paid for the services of Mesut Özil would have privately wondered whether they had bought a dud: £42 million attacking midfielders tend not to come with an extended service warranty

After a messy Christmas gave way to a scrappy New Year, perhaps these are early signs that the playmaker is rediscovering his lust for the fight

One of the theories about Özil’s curious period of hibernation was that his body was unused to the unusual rigours of an English festive programme. The winter break is a long-standing element in the German domestic calendar, and well established, if shorter, in Spain, where he moved to play for Real Madrid in 2010

With hindsight, perhaps that was a red herring. One of the more heart-stirring moments of the first half on Saturday was the breakdown of an Everton breakaway led by Kevin Mirallas. Mirallas is no slouch, and yet for 60 yards he was matched stride for stride by Özil: one attacking, one defending, both sprinting like cheetahs. The No 11s on their backs might as well have been speed stripes. Özil won the duel, got the tackle in. Nobody who saw those few seconds could question his fettle

Now, as Wenger explained, the strain had been mental, not physical … In this direction, however, lie further questions. Fairly or unfairly, the tag that still attaches itself to Özil is that too often, he does not influence the biggest games against the best opposition.

As absurd as this may seem for a man who has scored the winning goal in a German Cup final, made the match-winning assist in El Clásico and scored in a Euro 2012 semi-final, the second leg against Bayern Munich on Tuesday offers a chance to set the record straight

Perhaps the comparison with his former team-mate Cristiano Ronaldo is unfair, as too was the assertion of his former Germany under-21 coach Horst Hrubesch in 2009 that ‘Our Messi is Özil’. But the suspicion remains that had Ronaldo missed a crucial penalty in the opening stages of a Champions League game, he would simply have gone and won three more. If Arsenal are going to bang their fist on Europe’s top table, they need Özil to improve into that sort of player. And Wenger believed there was scope for him to do so

History may well blame media schizophrenia for Özil’s tribulations this season: build ‘em up to knock ‘em down to build ‘em up again. But it was not the media booing Özil as he played for Germany in Stuttgart in midweek, but his home fans
