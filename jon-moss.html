[Jon Moss makes a cappuccino behind the counter of The Vinyl Whistle, his record shop-cum-café in the Headingley district of Leeds, renowned for its vibrant nightlife and its student scene] You go onto a streaming site, they have all the essentials that you might want to listen to and then you don’t listen to the album in its entirety. I think people have moved away from listening to full albums, they just listen to random songs and I think there’s so much missed because of that

[But record stores give something back to the community they serve] If I want to hear about a song straight away, I might listen to it on streaming, but I’ll always want to own it on vinyl. There’s nothing quite like walking into a record shop, having a look through the racks, and finding something you didn’t know you were looking for. Or, similarly, finding something you’d been looking for, for a long time

[The indie stuff, Jon concedes, is more up his street] Whenever I get a chance and my wife’s away, I can probably sneak about 30 records at a time. I’ve got a bag that carries that

[On field, in stoppages between play, there might be a little conversation between the official and one of the players about a certain gig or a certain song] It’s as if you would have a conversation when you’re making a coffee at work.
