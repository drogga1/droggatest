Miroslav Klose
Gender Male
Ethnic German
Job German Footballer
Desc Born to a footballing father who played for Auxerre in France (the club’s second mention in this illustrious list) and a mother who was an international handball player, it’s not surprising that Miroslav Klose had professional sport in his blood. It’s just surprising it took so long to show. Into his 20s Klose was still struggling to break into the first team of lower-league FC 08 Homburg in Germany, but once he arrived at FC Kaiserslautern in 1999 things began to look up, with a Bundesliga debut there, a move to Werder Bremen in 2004 and then a 2007 move to Bayern Munich, where he finally achieved top honours with the Bundesliga title just shy of his 30th birthday in 2007-8

[Miroslav Klose praised the home fans following die Mannschaft’s 3-0 victory over Turkey in Berlin] We are all glad that we won the game. We must compliment the fans - we heard them. Why do I score for Germany and not Bayern Munich I ask myself every time. Here I have more luck, which is lacking when I play for Bayern. 2011 03 24 Retrieve

[Germany’s Miroslav Klose Sets Sights On Gerd Mueller’s Goal Record & 2014 World Cup Appearance] It’s a great joy when I think back on it. It was a fascinating time and a huge experience. First match, first goal - it was like a dream, like a fairy tale. The record is my big goal. Maybe I’ll have it before Euro 2012. It is something special that my anniversary game takes place at the Betzenberg [in Kaiserslautern]. There I made it big. The club is close to my heart. The stadium will be full… I’m sure the atmosphere will be good

I want to play in the national team as long as possible, and if I’m still healthy in 2012 after the European Championship, I would like to continue. The 2014 World Cup in Brazil is an attractive target

I feel fit and just have to see from year to year how long my body will last

[With his Bayern contract set to expire at season’s end, Klose has revealed that he will first speak to his current club, but that he could imagine moving elsewhere] I have always said that Bayern is my first contact, because although I am currently only a substitute, I cannot be completely blind. I could imagine anything, [to] renew, move within the Bundesliga or abroad.

[All-time top scorer Miroslav Klose talks about what he thinks makes the complete forward in the modern game] Personally, I always think that players like Diego Forlan are fantastic.
