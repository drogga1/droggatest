[From Pogba to Mourinho: Man Utd’s 15 most ill-fated decisions of the Ed Woodward era] David Moyes was sacked just 10 months into a six year-contract and replaced on an interim basis by Ryan Giggs with the reigning champions United languishing in seventh place in the Premier League standings

[Hiring Louis van Gaal] Louis van Gaal led United back into the Champions League, in 2015, and lifted the FA Cup the following year. However, even a Wembley win over Crystal Palace wasn’t enough to spare him from the sack. His painfully slow style of possession-orientated football had long become a source of immense frustration among the Old Trafford faithful, with United netting just 49 goals on their way to a disappointing fifth-placed finish in 2015-16

[Signing Angel Di Maria] With three goals and a swag of assists in his first five Premier League games, it looked like the Angel Di Maria was already on his way to becoming an Old Trafford legend. But Di Maria did not score for the club again, as it quickly became clear he wasn’t enjoying his time in Manchester. The player wanted to leave by the end of his first season and eventually secured a passage to Paris Saint-Germain, where he still remains today

[Giving up on Memphis Depay and Wilfried Zaha] Since leaving Old Trafford, both players have developed significantly, with Lyon ace Depay having become a key man for Netherlands, and Palace star Zaha now regarded as one of the most dangerous dribblers in the Premier League
