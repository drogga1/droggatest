2020 04 14 Retrieve

[The reasons behind Gylfi Sigurdsson’s Everton decline as former Swansea City star’s £45m transfer value plummets] This has been a whirlwind campaign in many respects for Gylfi Sigurdsson, but certainly not in the way he would have liked. The Iceland international has gone from Everton’s joint top scorer and being one of the first names on the teamsheet. to a real battle to even be in the starting 11.

What must be said is that things clearly haven’t gone the midfielder’s way, especially with a change in formation that Carlo Ancelotti has brought to the table. There’s no longer a natural place for Sigurdsson in the Blues system. He’s been forced to adapt in recent weeks and months, but even before then his form wasn’t at a necessary level. Alex Iwobi was often looking more comfortable in the attacking midfield role

Being Everton’s club-record signing comes with its own amount of pressure, clearly. Sigurdsson more than lived up to it last term, but he’s certainly struggled more and more this time around. Certain sections of supporters won’t be surprised and won’t argue with rumours linking the midfielder with a move in the summer as his place in Ancelotti’s current system looks to be swallowed up. If that happens, Everton will no doubt have to accept a loss on their investment
